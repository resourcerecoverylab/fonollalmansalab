Treatment & Treatment and resource recovery from hydrothermal liquefaction aqueous by-product

Funding: U.S. Department of Energy , Water Research Foundation, GLWA

Hydrothermal Liquefaction (HTL) is a chemical process that transform organic wastes into biocrude, a precursor for transportation fuels. However along with the Biocrude, HTL also produces a medium-high strength aqueous byproduct (HTL-aq). To ensure HTL implementation it is crucial that the treatment of HTL-aq is well understood and sustainable. Under this project we are using anaerobic digestion and chemical processes to recover biogas and ammonia. We are also evaluating the treatment of HTL-aq in bioreactors that simulate the activated sludge processes of WWRF.

Evaluating transformation and fate of PFAS during hydrothermal liquefaction of sewage sludge

Funding: U.S. Department of Energy

Per- and polyfluoroalkayl substances (PFAS) are emerging contaminants that are threatening the application of biosolids for agricultural purposes. Hydrothermal Liquefaction could provide a solution for the removal of PFAS. For this project we are evaluating the transformation and fate of PFAS in a pilot and continuous HTL reactor from Genifuel. We are measuring PFAS in the influent and all the effluents of HTL (biocrude, gas, liquid and solid effluents).

Evaluating nitrification inhibition by propylene glycol in Austin Water Resource Recovery Facility

Funding: City of Austin (AW)

In this collaboration with Austin Water we are using bench scale bioreactors to simulate Austin Water secondary treatment and study the inhibition of nitrifying bacteria by propylene glycol.

Unleashing Anaerobic Fungi in Anaerobic Digestion

Funding: University of Texas at Austin

However, the conversion of lignocellulose to biofuels requires costly and environmental damaging pretreatments to make plant material bioavailable. Anaerobic fungi, belonging to the phylum Neocallimastigomycota, can provide a solution for this.
